# Voice Coding: The Category I'm Naming Because Vibe Coding Won't Last

*By Amen Ra — April 15, 2026*

---

This week, two things happened in AI that most people connected as news items. I want to connect them as a pattern.

First: leaked footage showed Anthropic building a full-stack app builder inside Claude — a direct competitor to Lovable and Replit. Second: Anthropic is exploring custom AI chips, right behind OpenAI and Meta. Top of the stack and bottom of the stack, same week.

Here is what that pattern actually means for anyone building on top of foundation models.

**The moment you crack meaningful revenue in a software category above a foundation model, that foundation model sees you, builds your product into their platform, and prices you out.** You have a window — maybe three years — before you surface for air and become a target. The speaker I watched this morning called it the submarine strategy. He is right.

Vibe coding is the first category to get submerged. Lovable was the fastest company in history to $100M ARR. Replit raised $400M at a $9B valuation last month. Cursor is everywhere. And now all three of them are in Anthropic's crosshairs, because text-prompt-to-app is exactly the kind of thin differentiation a foundation model can replicate and bundle in a weekend.

I think the survivors of this wave are the products that live in a category **the foundation model cannot replicate cheaply.** Voice. Persona. Team. Culture.

So I'm giving that category a name.

## Voice Coding

**Voice Coding** *(noun):*

> The practice of building software, content, or workflows by conversing in real time with a team of two or more parallel AI agents, each with distinct voice, name, persona, and specialty, coordinated on a shared message bus, producing output in parallel under the direction of a single human operator.

I coined the term on April 15, 2026. I'm publishing the definition openly because I want the category to exist and grow — and because I want the record to show where it came from.

Voice Coding is not vibe coding. Vibe coding is *"text prompt, get an app."* Voice Coding is *"talk to a team you designed, and they build for you, in parallel, each one speaking in their own voice."*

The differences matter:

- **Voice-first.** You speak. Your team speaks back. Text is a fallback, not the interface.
- **Parallel.** Multiple named agents work simultaneously on different parts of what you're building. Not one silent worker. Not sequential.
- **Personalized.** You name the agents. You choose or clone the voices. You write the personalities. Your team is *yours* — not a generic worker bee.
- **Cultural.** Agents can carry heritage, accent, cadence, and story. You can build a team from your late grandmother's voice, your cofounder's voice, or personas you admire. This is the line foundation models cannot cross without inventing a culture first.

## Why This Can't Be Bundled

Anthropic can add a "prompt-to-app" button to Claude in a weekend. They cannot ship *your team* with *your voices* talking to you in parallel.

That is not a technical limitation. It is a brand and cultural limitation. Foundation model companies sell neutrality. Voice Coding sells specificity. The day Anthropic tries to launch a Voice Coding product, they will have to invent a persona, a voice, and a culture — and corporate AI companies cannot do that quickly. It takes decades of lived experience, and a willingness to be specific instead of universal.

That window is the moat.

## And Voice Products Need a New Kind of Spec

While I was naming the category, I kept hitting the same problem on product teams: our PRDs describe what the product does but say nothing about how it sounds. They are silent on voice register, pacing, tone, off-script handling, and what the voice will never say.

So I coined another term: **VRD — Voice Requirements Document.**

A VRD is a sibling to the PRD. It defines the voice persona, greeting scripts per surface, branching logic for user signals, prohibited phrases, and voice synthesis parameters. Any serious voice-first product should ship one.

The canonical VRD template is public and free to use, at the same repository where I am publishing this article:
